The Diagnosis
The Gap Is Not What You Think
The path from $500K to $1M is not a marketing budget problem. It is an architecture problem.

Greenbelt Event Rentals is a fundamentally sound business. Five-star reviews. Transparent pricing. Owner-direct contact. A genuine service reputation in a market where the dominant competitor just went through a disruptive acquisition and is hemorrhaging customer service complaints.

The business currently generates $500K per year through Google Ads, organic search, and word of mouth. All three channels work. The problem is structural: there is no compounding mechanism beneath them. Every dollar of revenue is hunted individually. A customer books, receives great service, and then disappears from the funnel forever — with no system to bring them back, no system to ask for a referral, and no system to capture leads that arrived after hours.

The following projection assumes no change to service quality, no new inventory, and no increase in Google Ads spend. Revenue Leakage
Where Money Is Currently Escaping
Five specific gaps. Each one is an untapped asset, not a weakness.
Gap 01 — The Dormant List Highest Leverage

A 1,000-contact email list of people who already raised their hand is sitting untouched. Industry benchmarks recover 10–25% of dormant contacts through reactivation campaigns. At 10% reactivation at $600 average order value: $60,000 in 90 days. Email ROI averages $36–$42 per dollar spent. This is not a new customer acquisition problem — it is a dormant asset activation problem.

Gap 02 — No Repeat Revenue System Critical

Event rentals are cyclical. Corporate clients book quarterly. Families host annual events. There is currently no structured follow-up system converting one-time customers into repeat clients. 20% of 500 annual clients rebooking at $600 average = $60,000/year at near-zero acquisition cost.

Gap 03 — No Vendor Referral Network Medium Priority

Austin's event industry runs entirely on vendor referrals. Photographers, caterers, florists, and planners are constantly asked "who do you recommend for tent rentals?" Greenbelt has no formal program in place. A 5–10% referral commission with 15–20 Austin vendor partners = 100 additional bookings per year.

Gap 04 — No Off-Season Strategy Medium Priority

Austin's event calendar clusters in spring and fall. Summer and winter inventory sits underutilized. Corporate holiday events (Nov–Dec) and Q1 kickoffs (Jan–Feb) are underserved by Greenbelt's current marketing. Targeted corporate outreach for these windows adds $45,000+ annually.

Gap 05 — Review Velocity Growth Lever

Perfect 5-star rating with minimal review volume. Competitors dominate Google, The Knot, and WeddingWire with review count — the primary organic discovery mechanism for this category. An automated post-event review request sequence costs nothing in media and directly reduces Google Ads dependency over 6–12 months.

Requirements
What This Actually Requires
Three honest inputs. No surprises.
Time
20–30 hours of Tom's input during the 30-day Build phase. Primarily account setup, data export, and email approval. Once live: 4–6 hours/month for email review and booking tracking. The system is built to free Tom's time — not consume it.
Budget
Mailchimp Standard (~$20/mo) + CRM integration ($50–$150/mo) + AI intake agent ($200–$350/mo) + review automation tool ($50–$100/mo). Total: $320–$620/month in tools. Against $40,000–$80,000/month in projected new revenue, this is a sub-2% cost of revenue.
Commitment
90 days of consistent execution. The single failure mode is partial implementation — the list without the sequences, or the vendor program without the follow-through. Complete deployment is the only deployment that compounds.
